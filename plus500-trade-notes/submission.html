Plus500 CFD · Trade Notes

Product rationale & desktop wireframes · Home assignment · June 2026

The problem & business impact

About 80% of retail CFD accounts at Plus500 lose money.1 The main reason is not lack of knowledge, it is behavior: traders enter on emotion (fear of missing out, and revenge after a loss), don't learn from their mistakes, and repeat them until the account is gone. A trader who loses his money leaves.

In 2025 Plus500 acquired ~104,500 new customers, yet its active base actually shrank (254,138 to ~242,000).2 The company spends heavily on acquisition just to stand still. Trade Notes is a retention play: a trader who learns and survives longer stays a customer, which is a direct saving in acquisition cost and a lift in lifetime value.

Who it is for

Any trader who wants to improve, beginner or experienced. For example, the trader who keeps buying back in right after a loss to win it back. He knows it is a mistake, but emotion beats logic at the moment of entry, and he never stops to see the pattern repeat.

Why traders will actually use it

Most journals die because logging is a separate chore. Here the note is captured inside the trade flow, in one tap, never required. The AI does the analysis, so even short notes pay off, and the trader sees value fast, which builds the habit.

The solution: a simple core, then an AI layer

The core ships on its own and answers the brief directly: one note that travels with each trade. An AI layer then learns from those notes.

  1. The note thread (this is the MVP). One note that accompanies the trade at every stage: planning, entry, while open, closing, and summary. Free text, or a one-tap tag. This alone is a complete, shippable Trade Notes feature.
  2. The AI layer (the next phase, what makes it intelligent). It reads the notes and gives insights and recommendations about you (for example "most of your losses mention 'gut feeling'", or "before a reversal, wait for confirmation"), and brings the right note back at a similar moment. It looks inward at your behavior, not at the market, and never gives buy/sell calls. It complements +AI Bites, which looks outward.
  3. Flexibility through simple settings. A few toggles let each trader decide how present the tool is, from full guidance to just a free note box. It never blocks or forces.
  4. Native to Plus500. Everything sits on existing screens (order ticket, open position, closed positions), so it feels natural and is cheap to build.

Deliberately out of scope for now: full analytics dashboards, a daily-loss stop, and importing an existing journal (Excel/CSV) to seed the AI. The first release stays focused on the note itself.

How we will know it works: % of active traders who adopt Trade Notes (feature adoption, not just overall retention) · share of trades that include a note (depth of use) · fewer repeated emotional entries · longer active lifespan per trader · improved 30 and 90 day retention.

✨ What the AI learns from your notes
💡Most of your losses mention "gut feeling" or entering too early.
💡Your winning trades almost always mention waiting for confirmation.
✅Recommendation: before entering a reversal, wait for a confirmation candle to close. This is the pattern that recurs in your winning trades.

The AI reads your notes and reflects patterns about you, not the market. The quick-tags give it a clean structured signal alongside the free text, so insights hold even when notes are short or messy. It advises on your process only; it never predicts the market or places buy/sell calls (important under CFD regulation).
